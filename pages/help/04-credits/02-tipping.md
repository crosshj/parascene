## What tipping is

Tipping lets you send credits directly to a creator to support work you like. It’s a lightweight way to say “this was
worth something to me” without leaving the app.

Tips move credits from your balance to the creator’s balance. Creators can then use those credits in the product
(for their own creations, boosts, or servers) depending on what’s available to them.

Tipping keeps value with creators and helps the community grow. For other ways to use credits, see
[Run a server](/help/credits/run-a-server).

## How to tip

You can tip from:

- **Creation detail** – On an eligible creation, use the **Tip** or **Tip creator** action and choose an amount from the
  options shown.  
- **Creator profile** – When tipping from a profile is available, use the tip action near the creator’s info.

You’ll see:

- Your **current credit balance**.
- One or more **suggested amounts** (you can pick one of these).
- A clear confirmation step before the tip is sent.

Once you confirm, the tip is applied immediately and the creator’s credit balance is updated.

## Limits, safety, and abuse prevention

To keep tipping healthy and reduce abuse (for example, spam accounts trying to move value around), we apply a few
protections:

- **Waiting period for new accounts** – New users may see a short delay before tipping is enabled.
- **Activity checks** – In some cases we may limit tipping if we detect unusual or abusive patterns.

This restriction does **not** apply to upgraded accounts — if you upgrade your plan, you can usually tip right away.
See [Upgrade your plan](/help/credits/upgrade-plan) for how upgrading works, or learn more on the [pricing page](/pricing).

If you ever think something looks wrong with tipping on your account, reach out via the in‑app support or contact
information listed in the help center.
