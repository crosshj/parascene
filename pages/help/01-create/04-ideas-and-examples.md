## Stuck on what to create?

It's normal to feel a bit blocked when you're staring at an empty prompt box. This page gives you concrete ideas and
prompt patterns you can copy, tweak, and build on.

Use these as starting points rather than rules — changing a few words is often enough to make something feel like yours.

## Quick ideas to try

### Profile and social

- **Friendly avatar**  
  `A friendly portrait of a person with short hair, soft lighting, simple background, illustration style`

- **Bold profile picture**  
  `High-contrast portrait with strong shadows, neon accents, cyberpunk city in the background`

- **Header or banner**  
  `Wide landscape with mountains and a glowing sunset sky, minimal foreground, cinematic lighting`

### Places and scenes

- **A real place**  
  `A quiet side street in Tokyo at night, warm shop lights, light rain, reflections on the pavement`

- **A memory**  
  `A cozy living room at night with a reading chair, warm lamp, and stacks of books, soft focus`

- **A dream location**  
  `A floating island with waterfalls spilling into the clouds, distant planets in the sky, fantasy illustration`

### Characters and creatures

- **Original character**  
  `A fantasy ranger with a green cloak and bow, standing in a misty forest, detailed illustration`

- **Creature design**  
  `A small dragon made of glass and light, hovering over a hand, studio lighting, dark background`

- **Stylized portrait**  
  `Portrait in the style of a 90s anime, bright colors, expressive eyes, simple background`

### Abstract and style experiments

- **Abstract shapes**  
  `Abstract geometric shapes in complementary colors, clean vector style, white background`

- **Texture study**  
  `Close-up of colorful oil paint strokes on canvas, high detail, macro photography`

- **Mood piece**  
  `An image that feels calm and slow, soft blue and green tones, minimal composition`

## Prompt patterns that work well

Most good prompts follow a simple pattern:

> **[subject] + [style] + [mood/lighting] + [extra detail]**

Here are a few examples:

- `A fox (subject) in a forest clearing, watercolor illustration (style), early morning light (mood/lighting), soft mist in the background (extra detail)`
- `City skyline at night (subject), long-exposure photography (style), bright neon signs (mood/lighting), light trails from cars (extra detail)`
- `A cozy reading nook (subject), isometric pixel art (style), warm golden light (mood/lighting), plants and stacks of books (extra detail)`

Try filling in your own:

- Someone or something you care about (a hobby, a pet, a favorite place).
- A style you like (photo, painting, pixel art, anime, poster, logo).
- A mood (calm, dramatic, nostalgic, energetic).

You don't have to get it perfect — you can always generate, see what comes out, then refine.

## Using @mentions in prompts

One of the most powerful parts of parascene is **@mentions**. When you type something like `@name` in a prompt, the app
can use that creator’s public profile and character description to influence the result.

Use @mentions when you want to:

- Create **with** a friend or favorite creator’s style or character.
- Make variations that keep a **consistent character** over many images.
- Let others build on your own character description when they @mention you.

Tips:

- Start typing `@` and a few letters on the create page to see suggestions.
- Keep your own **character description** short and concrete so other people’s @mentions of you work well.

Example prompts:

- `@alex in a cozy cafe, sketchbook open on the table, warm evening light, digital painting`
- `Portrait of @riley exploring a neon city at night, anime style`
- `Group photo of @jordan and @sam on a rooftop at sunset, cinematic lighting`

You can also browse creations that mention a given @username from their profile (Mentions tab) or via discovery routes
in the app.

## If you're feeling creatively blocked

If you're not sure where to start:

- **Remix something familiar**  
  Take a movie, book, or game you like and describe a scene *in your own words* (no need to name it explicitly).

- **Start from a template**  
  Use the **Basic** create flow with a template and just change one or two details the first time.

- **Describe a real place**  
  Think of somewhere you know well and describe what it looks and feels like — time of day, weather, colors, and details.

- **Change only one variable**  
  Generate something simple, then try the same prompt with a new style (e.g. “pixel art”, “collage”, “poster”).

- **Borrow from your day**  
  Describe part of your day visually:  
  `A desk with a laptop, coffee mug, and headphones, soft morning light through the window`

If you ever get stuck, it's better to type *something small* and see what happens than to wait for the perfect idea.
You can always delete, remix, or keep iterating until something clicks.
